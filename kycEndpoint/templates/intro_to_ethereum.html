What is Ethereum?

So if you've made it here and are unfamiliar with Ethereum, let me be the first to welcome you! Before we get into some specifics though, please be advised to FULLY understand the risks involved with placing funds on the Ethereum network. While the technology behind Ethereum is incredibly secure and robust, most errors occur due to user misunderstanding or error. Due to the nature of the underlying technology, mistakes cannot be reversed so be careful and understand what you are doing.

Ethereum is a popular general-purpose blockchain network that launched in 2015. You may be familiar with another blockchain network, Bitcoin, which has received a lot of attention on the internet and in the media. Bitcoin is a predecessor to the Ethereum network a has really led the way for blockchains adoption. The Bitcoin network is limited to simply payments though and Ethereum's biggest innovation was the creation "smart contracts". These smart contracts are really just simple programs that anyone can interact with on the Ethereum network. That is revolutionary! Now we have a decentralized global infrastructure that allows anyone, anywhere, at anytime to interact with a simple program and have the entire network come to agreement about how that interaction occured. Not only does everyone agree about how the interaction occured but it is also impossible to erase the existence of that interaction.

Simple use case

Imagine loaning a friend $10 and agreeing to a repayment date. Then your friend forgets and denies you ever loaned them the money. This type of interaction is the basis for real world contracts with lawyers but now using Ethereum you can loan you friend $10 and record the transaction and repayment date onto the network. Then come the date of repayment, you have an unchanging immutable record you can point back to when asking for collection. Additionally, you can have the contract compute the interest owed and enforce repercussions of what happens on default of payment. Building on top of this infrastructure, you might imagine a whole bunch of interactions between people that could benefit from the increased transparency and immutability of data that blockchains can provide.

Metamask is essentailly a user controlled wallet that allows users to interact with decentralized application (dApps) which are run using Ethereum smart contracts. Please follow all the instructions from Metamask to ensure you do not lose access to your Ethereum account.

How do I get Ether?

Once you have installed Metamask, the easiest way to fund your account is to purchase tokens from an exchange and send them to yourself. Below we provide a simple demonstration using Coinbase and purchasing funds with a credit card. However, this is not the only route and we encourage users to research alternative strategies to fund their wallets, if they choose.

  • Once you have installed Metamask you will see the option to "Deposit" as shown

  • After clicking, "Deposit" you are presented with a few options for how to fund your account. For now we are going to choose Coinbase.

  • Fill in the amount you wish to purchase and an email address which will be used to access your Coinbase account.

  • Fill in your name, state, and agree to the terms.

  • You will then be sent a confirmation code to the email you provided which you must enter into the field in order to verify your identity.

  • Enter the information needed to confirm your identity and create your Coinbase account.

  • Choose your source of funds. Follow the prompts if you would like to link a bank account but for the purposes of this walkthrough we'll choose a credit/debit card.

  • Enter your card details and click "Continue"
  • Confrim your purchase amount
  • Congratulations! You have just purchased Ether. Your funds should appear in Metamask shortly. Once there you can return to the pre-sale homepage and participate in the sale.

Is it safe?

The blockchain technology which underlies Ethereum is incredibly robust and secure but due to the newness of the field many scammers have devised clever ways to trick user out of their funds. While the tips below are not an exhaustive list of steps you should take to protect yourself, they do serve as a good starting point for skepticism. Remember to always double check the address before you send funds and never follow prompts if you are unsure or suspicious of their origins or intent.

  • Topl will never contact you on social media with a naked wallet address and ask you to send funds to it. If you receive such a request please email info@topl.me and do not make the transaction
  • Whenever you are not using MetaMask to check your balance or make a transaction log out of it
  • Be aware of fake pop ups that prompt you to send funds via your MetaMask. Always double check where you are sending funds and NEVER click "Confirm" if you are unsure
  • Never enter your MetaMask password, private key, or seed phrase into a third-party website. You should only input your password, private key, or seed phrase into the official application.
  • If an offer appears "to good to be true" it probably is and is looking for some way to steal your funds.

Once more, welcome to the world of Ethereum and blockchains! This last bit may have seemed worrying but is only meant as a caution and to remind you that YOU are in control of your funds nad therefore responsible for where they are sent.

Be safe, don't panic, and always bring a towel. Thank you for supporting Topl!
